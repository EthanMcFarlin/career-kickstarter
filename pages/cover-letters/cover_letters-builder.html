Template Selection

As a means to appropriately fill the content of your cover letter, we have laid out a series of criteria around which you can base your writting.

Your employment history may fall into more than one header, in which case we would recommend selecting whichever template you feel best markets you as an individual.

An additional layer of abstraction atop each header is a label referring to the use case under a particular category. Your choice of label should match the proffesionality established in the job listing.

    Enthusiastic

    My name is [insert name]. I am delighted to have the opportunity to apply for the [position] role at [company]. Upon reading through the job listing, I’ve come to understand you’re looking for an enthusiastic applicant with a will to engage fully in the role and continually advance their skillset. With that said, and given these requirements, I believe myself to be a suitable candidate for the job.

    I’m currently enrolled at [insert high school], where I show a passion for [2-3 subjects] and have repeatedly challenged myself by taking [course types]. Throughout the course of my academic career, I have managed to accumulate a [insert GPA if above 3.5], which follows suit with my teachers’ constant appraisal as a [1-3 soft skills]. Whilst still enrolled at school, I’ve engaged in school-wide and even community groups, which include [insert extracurricular activities]. Such involvement has instilled a strong sense of affinity with my community and a desire to collaborate with others. Whether through my involvement in extracurriculars or my devotion to studiousness, I have proven to be a [1-3 soft skills], all of which are qualities I hope to leverage at [company].

    I look forward to the possibilities of future collaboration, especially when considering [insert company] need for strongly-motivated and team-oriented workers. Feel free to contact me at [insert PHONE] or via email at [insert EMAIL] to further the discussion.

    Thank you for your consideration.
